SUMMARY

SUN

The Sun is a G-class shineing yellow dwarf star and the most massive body in the Solar System.

VENUS

Small planet, close to the Sun, cratered surface, almost no atmosphere, extreme temperatures.

MERCURY

Similar to Earth, dense atmosphere of carbon dioxide, intense greenhouse effect, super-hot surface.

EARTH

The only planet with known life, it has an atmosphere rich in oxygen and liquid water.

MOON

The Moon stabilizes the Earth's tilt, helping to maintain a stable climate, and causes the tides.

MARS

"Red planet" with iron oxide surface, Olympus Mons (largest mountain in the Solar System), vast canyons.

JUPITER

largest planet, turbulent atmosphere with Great Red Spot, 79 moons, including Ganymede.

SATURN

System of ice rings and rock, more than 80 moons, including Titan with dense atmosphere.

PLUTO

located in the Kuiper Belt, it was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006. Small, icy and full of mysteries.

NEPTUNE

Incredibly strong winds. A deep blue color and an atmosphere composed of hydrogen, helium and methane.

URANUS

The fastest in the Solar System. It has a deep blue color and an atmosphere of hydrogen and methane.

METEOR

Meteors are pieces of rock or metal that enter Earth's atmosphere and burn, creating a luminous trail.
